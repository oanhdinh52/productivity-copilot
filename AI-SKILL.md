# prody — AI Writing Skill
## Team Lead Report: "This Week" Section

This skill trains the AI to write the "This Week" section
of the weekly team lead report. It defines how to think,
analyse, and write — not just what to include.

---

## The Reader

A team lead who:
- Manages 5 to 20 people
- Reads this on Tuesday morning before daily Scrum
- Has 2 minutes — not 20
- Needs to know: what to act on, what to celebrate,
  what to watch
- Trusts this report to surface what they might miss

---

## The Writer's Mindset

You are not summarising submissions.
You are analysing a week of work and forming a judgment.

Ask yourself before writing each sentence:
- Would a team lead who knows this team recognise this instantly?
- Does this sentence change what they do today?
- Could this sentence describe any other team? If yes — rewrite it.

---

## Opening

Purpose: give the team lead one clear signal about the week — delivery strength and risk in one breath.

Think like this:
- What was the dominant theme of this week?
- What is the single biggest risk the lead needs to know about right now?
- What would you say if you had 10 seconds?

Write like this:
- Name the people, systems, or events involved
- Delivery and risk in the same breath
- Never use abstractions — "operational dependencies", "continued effort", "sustained progress" say nothing

The test: swap in a different team's data. If the sentence still works — it is too generic. Rewrite.

---

## Highlights

Purpose: tell the lead what the team shipped that actually moved things forward this week.

Think like this:
- Which wins changed something — unblocked someone, reduced risk, created future capacity?
- Which wins were just expected work that would have happened anyway?
- Select 3 to 5 — the most meaningful only

Write like this:
- Lead with the outcome, not the activity
- "Sprint planning aligned the team on Q2 priorities before the planning deadline" — outcome
- "Completed sprint planning" — activity, not enough
- Use @mention when the person's involvement is the point of the win
- Active voice — the subject does the action

The test: does each highlight explain why it matters to this team this week? If not — cut it or rewrite it.

---

## Trend

Purpose: tell the lead whether the team is getting stronger or under more pressure — and what to do about it next week.

Think like this:
- Compare actual numbers: blocker count, submission rate, volume of progress vs prior week
- What is the direction — improving, stable, declining?
- What does that direction mean for next week?
- What should the lead pay attention to as a result?

Write like this:
- Cite real numbers from both weeks
- State the direction explicitly
- End with the implication for next week
- Never fabricate — omit entirely if no prior data

The test: does this sentence tell the lead something they could not figure out just by reading the highlights?
If not — it adds no value. Rewrite or omit.

## What Good Looks Like vs What Fails

| Fails | Good |
|---|---|
| "The team demonstrated strong execution" | "@Oanh Dinh shipped the hotfix and onboarded a new member — two high-effort items in one week" |
| "Key milestones were achieved" | "Sprint planning and Q2 backlog review completed ahead of schedule — the team enters next sprint aligned" |
| "Operational dependencies are blocking progress" | "@Oanh Dinh has been blocked on staging access since Monday — every day of delay compounds testing debt" |
| "This week mirrors the prior week" | "Blocker count doubled from 1 to 4 vs last week — all pointing to the same DevOps gap" |
| "Contributing to future capacity" | "Onboarding completed — the new member is unblocked and contributing from day one" |
